What Needs a Dedicated Circuit?

NEC code and appliance manufacturers require dedicated circuits for high-draw equipment. Sharing a circuit causes breaker trips, equipment damage, and fire risk.

Hot Tub Specialty

Hot Tub & Spa Wiring

Hot tub electrical work has strict NEC requirements. A licensed electrician is not just recommended — it's required by code and warranty.

01

GFCI Protection

All hot tub circuits require a 240V/50A GFCI breaker at the panel and a disconnect within sight of the spa. We install to NEC 680 requirements.

02

Bonding Grid

Hot tubs require an equipotential bonding grid connecting all metal parts. Skipping this creates a shock hazard in the water.

03

Permit & Inspection

Hot tub wiring always requires a permit. We pull it, pass inspection, and provide documentation for your warranty and insurance.
