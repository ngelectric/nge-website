FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions we hear about home EV charger installation.

Q What type of EV charger do you install?

We install Level 2 (240V) home chargers, which charge 5–10 times faster than a standard 120V outlet. We can install a NEMA 14-50 outlet for plug-in chargers (like the Tesla Mobile Connector or ChargePoint Flex) or hardwire a dedicated unit directly. We work with all major brands including Tesla, ChargePoint, Emporia, JuiceBox, and Clipper Creek.

Q Do I need a permit to install an EV charger at home?

In most cities and counties — including Temecula, Murrieta, and across San Diego County — a permit is required for Level 2 EV charger installations. Permits ensure the work is inspected and up to code, which matters for home insurance and resale. We handle the entire permit process on your behalf so you don't have to.

Q How long does EV charger installation take?

Most installations are completed in 2–4 hours. More complex jobs — such as running conduit through finished walls, exterior routing, or panel work — may take a full day. We'll give you an accurate time estimate when we assess your home.

Q Can I install a charger if my electrical panel is older or at capacity?

Yes, in most cases. If your panel is full or undersized, we can add a tandem breaker, load management device, or recommend a panel upgrade depending on your total electrical load. We'll assess your panel during the free estimate and let you know exactly what's needed before any work is done.

Q Which electric vehicle brands and models do you support?

All of them. Every EV sold in the US — Tesla, Ford F-150 Lightning, Chevrolet Bolt, Rivian, BMW, Hyundai IONIQ, Kia EV6, Toyota bZ4X, and more — charges via a standard J1772 connector or the newer NACS port. A Level 2 home charger installation works for every make and model. We also install NEMA 14-50 outlets for maximum future flexibility.
