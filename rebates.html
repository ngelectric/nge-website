Rebates & Incentives for Southern California Homeowners

Panel upgrades, EV chargers, and ADU electrical may qualify for significant rebates and tax credits.

Get Free Estimate
Note: Incentive programs change frequently and funding is limited. Always verify current availability directly with your utility provider or tax advisor. New Ground Electric shares this information as a courtesy — we are not affiliated with any rebate program.
Riverside County

SCE Customers

Southern California Edison serves Temecula, Murrieta, Menifee, Winchester, French Valley, Wildomar, Lake Elsinore, Fallbrook, and surrounding Riverside County communities.

Charge Ready Home Rebate

Up to $4,200

SCE offers rebates to eligible customers who upgrade their electrical panel to support a Level 2 EV charger installation.

  • ✓Income-qualified: up to $4,200 (100% of cost)
  • ✓Disadvantaged communities: up to $2,100
  • ✓Must plan EV charger within 180 days
  • ✓Single-family residences only
  • ✓Current panel must be under 200A
Learn More at SCE →

Federal Tax Credit (25C)

Up to 30% Back

The Inflation Reduction Act covers up to 30% of EV charger equipment and installation costs for most homeowners.

  • ✓Covers equipment and labor
  • ✓Annual limit of $1,000 for EV chargers
  • ✓Primary residence only
  • ✓No income requirement
  • ✓Consult a tax professional to confirm

San Diego County

SDG&E Customers

San Diego Gas & Electric serves Poway, Escondido, San Marcos, Oceanside, Carlsbad, Encinitas, Rancho Santa Fe, Del Mar, La Jolla, and surrounding communities.

SDG&E Panel & EV Charger Rebates

SDG&E does not currently offer a utility-specific rebate for residential panel upgrades or EV charger installation. SDG&E customers can still benefit from the federal 25C tax credit and state programs below.

Check SDG&E's current offerings →

Federal Tax Credit (25C)

Up to 30% Back

Available to all homeowners regardless of utility. Covers up to 30% of EV charger equipment and installation — up to $1,000 annually.

IRS Guidance →

CalHFA ADU Grant

Up to $40,000

Covers pre-development ADU costs including design fees, surveys, and permit fees. Monitor CalHFA for current funding availability.

Available to All Southern California Homeowners

Federal EV Charger Credit

30% — Up to $1,000/year

The 25C credit covers equipment and labor for Level 2 charger installation. No income requirement. Applies to SCE and SDG&E customers. Consult a tax professional.

Pre-Owned EV Rebate

Up to $4,000

Both SCE and SDG&E offer rebates for pre-owned EV purchases. $1,000 standard, up to $4,000 income-qualified. Apply within 180 days of purchase.

Stack Your Savings

Combine Multiple Incentives

SCE customers doing a panel upgrade for an EV charger could combine the Charge Ready Home rebate ($4,200), federal 25C credit ($1,000), and pre-owned EV rebate (up to $4,000). Ask Mario during your estimate.
